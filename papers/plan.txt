Let me filter the ideas strictly from a deep learning POV, but still low-dependency on Arabic-specific knowledge.

🔑 Key observation from the paper
Most strong systems:

Use SSL speech encoders (Wav2Vec2, Whisper, XLS-R)

Then do phoneme prediction + alignment

Sometimes with light heads or partial fine-tuning

👉 So your best strategy:
Stay in representation learning space, not linguistic space

🧠 Best Deep-Learning Ideas (Low Arabic Knowledge Needed)
🥇 1. “Error = Representation Mismatch” (VERY clean idea)
Core idea
Instead of predicting phonemes:

Detect mispronunciation via embedding inconsistency

Method
Use pretrained model (Wav2Vec2 / Whisper encoder)

Extract frame-level embeddings

Compare:

Input audio embedding

Reference audio embedding (or TTS)

Compute:

cosine similarity per segment

or DTW distance

Low similarity → error

Why this is good
❌ No phoneme modeling

❌ No Arabic knowledge

✅ Pure deep learning geometry

Light improvement (publishable twist)
👉 Add multi-layer contrast

early layer = acoustic

late layer = linguistic

Then:

error_score = w1 * acoustic_diff + w2 * semantic_diff
Paper angle
“Multi-level representation discrepancy for mispronunciation detection”

🥈 2. Self-Supervised Anomaly Detection (No labels needed)
Core idea
Train model on correct recitation only, detect anomaly

Method
Use pretrained encoder (freeze it)

Train small model:

autoencoder OR

normalizing flow OR

Gaussian density

Train ONLY on correct speech

At test:

high reconstruction error → mispronunciation

Why strong
Matches problem nature:

correct speech = dominant

errors = anomaly

No Arabic needed
Model learns:

“what normal speech sounds like”

Light modification idea
👉 Instead of raw audio:

use Wav2Vec2 latent features

easier & stable

Paper angle
“Self-supervised anomaly detection for Quranic mispronunciation”

🥉 3. CTC Alignment WITHOUT phoneme knowledge
Core idea
Still use ASR—but ignore Arabic phoneme complexity

Method
Use pretrained ASR (Wav2Vec2 / Whisper)

Force decode with reference text

Extract:

alignment confidence

CTC posterior

Low confidence → error

Improvement (your contribution)
👉 Instead of raw confidence:

compute entropy / margin-based confidence

Example:

score = p(top1) - p(top2)
Why good
Already close to SOTA pipeline

Minimal modification

No phoneme engineering

Paper angle
“Confidence-based mispronunciation detection using pretrained ASR”

🧪 4. Contrastive Learning WITHOUT labels (nice DL idea)
Core idea
Teach model:

correct vs slightly perturbed audio

Method
Take correct audio

Create synthetic “errors”:

speed perturbation

pitch shift

small phoneme replacement via TTS

Train:

anchor = correct
positive = correct (augmented)
negative = corrupted
Use contrastive loss

Why strong
No annotation needed

Pure DL

Works even without Arabic knowledge

Paper angle
“Contrastive representation learning for pronunciation robustness”

🧠 5. Frame-level binary classifier (simplest DL baseline)
Core idea
Skip phoneme classification

Just:

frame → correct / incorrect

Method
Extract features (Wav2Vec2 frozen)

Train tiny classifier:

MLP / Transformer (2 layers)

Why good
Extremely simple

Matches evaluation (binary detection)

No linguistic features needed

Light improvement
👉 Add temporal smoothing:

CRF / temporal conv

Paper angle
“Direct frame-level mispronunciation detection using SSL features”

If your goal is highest accuracy, not simplicity, then the answer is:

Best bet: full fine-tuning of a large pretrained speech model + extra in-domain data

In the Iqra’Eval shared task, the best reported system was BAIC with F1 = 0.4726, ahead of Hafs2Vec at 0.4650. The organizers also note that the strongest systems relied on large pretrained encoders and that the top systems improved further by adding synthetic data or extra Qur’anic recitation audio.

So, from a “what tends to win” perspective, the pattern is:

large SSL/ASR backbone
→ full or substantial fine-tuning
→ domain adaptation to Qur’anic recitation
→ extra training data, especially synthetic or in-domain audio.

What model family to pick

The strongest families in this benchmark were:

Wav2Vec2 / Wav2Vec2-BERT / XLS-R
Whisper-large-v3

If you want the most accuracy-oriented route, I would rank them like this:

Option A — most accuracy-oriented

Wav2Vec2-BERT or XLS-R style backbone, fully fine-tuned for speech-to-phoneme
Why:

this matches the shared-task trend closely
top systems were not purely lightweight heads; they leaned on stronger adaptation and more data
Option B — strong and easier to engineer

Whisper-large-v3 adapted to speech-to-phoneme
Why:

already used by ANPLers in the task
strong multilingual prior
easier decoder behavior than building from scratch
Highest-accuracy training recipe

If I had to choose one method for you, it would be this:

Multi-stage fine-tuning with a large pretrained encoder

This is the most accuracy-looking recipe without requiring deep Arabic/Tajweed expertise.

Stage 1: generic Arabic speech adaptation

Take a large pretrained model and adapt it on available Arabic speech first.

Stage 2: Qur’anic / recitation domain adaptation

Fine-tune on Qur’anic-style recitation or closely matched formal recitation audio.

Stage 3: speech-to-phoneme fine-tuning on IqraEval

Train directly for the task target: phoneme sequence prediction or phoneme-level detection.

That strategy is strongly consistent with the shared-task trends, and one of the submitted systems is explicitly framed as domain adaptation with multi-stage fine-tuning.

The single most important add-on for accuracy

Not architecture.
Data.

The organizers explicitly say the top 2 approaches added:

synthetic data
or extra human Qur’anic recitation data

So if you want maximum performance, your method should be:

Backbone

Wav2Vec2-BERT / XLS-R / Whisper-large-v3

Training

full fine-tuning or near-full fine-tuning

Extra data
TTS-generated correct recitation
TTS-generated mispronounced recitation
extra real Qur’anic recitation audio

That is probably more important than small architectural tricks.

Best concrete method to propose

Here is the version I’d recommend as your “highest-accuracy” method:

Method: Multi-stage domain-adaptive Whisper/Wav2Vec2-BERT with synthetic mispronunciation augmentation

Pipeline:

Start from Whisper-large-v3 or Wav2Vec2-BERT
Adapt on Arabic speech
Adapt on Qur’anic recitation audio
Fine-tune for speech-to-phoneme
Add synthetic error generation
phoneme substitution
deletion
insertion
Decode phoneme sequence
Align prediction vs reference to produce MDD labels
Why this is good for you

It is accuracy-focused, but it still does not require deep Arabic NLP knowledge because:

you do not need hand-built phonological rules
you do not need Tajweed-engineering
you mostly need:
data pipeline
phoneme vocabulary
ASR fine-tuning skills
alignment post-processing

So this is much more “general speech DL” than “deep Arabic linguistics.”
